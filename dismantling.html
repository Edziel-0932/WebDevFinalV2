Dismantling Types

  • Manual Dismantling
  • Semi-Automated Dismantling
  • Automated Dismantling

Manual Dismantling

This involves workers physically separating components by hand, using basic tools like screwdrivers, pliers, and cutters.

  • Low-cost method
  • Requires safety precautions (gloves, goggles)
  • Time-consuming but flexible for different devices

Semi-Automated Dismantling

Combines human effort with machinery for efficiency. Machines help remove specific parts, while workers handle the rest.

  • Speeds up dismantling process
  • Safer handling of hazardous components
  • Requires training and equipment

Automated Dismantling

Uses specialized machines and robotics to completely dismantle electronic waste without human intervention.

  • Fast and consistent results
  • High initial cost for setup
  • Best for large-scale recycling plants
